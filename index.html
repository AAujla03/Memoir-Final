THE BEGINNING

11th Grade Discovery

I started making music when I was in 11th grade. At that time I didn't know anything about how to make music and just like everything I decided to teach myself how to make music so I can do something else as well that I love along with my other passions and past time things.

"I had no idea where to start, but I was determined to figure it out."

Finding Ableton

Naturally I researched on how to make music and what are the best DAW's (Digital Audio Workstations) are. Eventually I stumbled upon Ableton that stuck with me purely out of its ability to have vibrant colors that you can make to different audio tracks to differentiate them.

Exploring Sample Packs

I didn't know where to start, so I started looking up some more, I came across sample tracks and sample packs that were free for producers and websites that would provide them in bulk. So I had an idea to use sample tracks when I make music.

First Setup

My first computer was my Imac from mid 2010s, pretty old but the speakers were good so I stuck with it. Ableton ran surprisingly good on it. I started experimenting with the sample packs and eventually got hang of it. This took me about a week.

Distribution Research

I started thinking about how am I going to distribute these? I looked distribution companies and did my research, I found distrokid to be the most favorable. Great at the price it was and pretty nice options. So I went with it. Purchased it and then thought to actually get onto making music.

WHAT IF: You hadn't pursued music?

Reality

I followed my curiosity and taught myself music production, discovering a passion that would lead to unexpected success.

Alternate Path

I would have missed out on discovering a creative talent and the thrill of sharing my music with thousands of listeners worldwide.

FINDING A PARTNER

The Chance Meeting

I started making Rap/Hip-Hop beats at first and in the middle of making it, I found one my classmates who was new to the school and came when we started 11th grade to be an audio engineer and also a music producer. Naturally we hit it off and we had a lot in common.

"I told him about my plans and how I am making music and how I started. He was hesitant to do it at first."

The First Album

I made my complete album, designed the cover art and uploaded it to distrokid. My very first album that I made was up and I was overjoyed. I started telling all my friends and even posted about it on my insta.

The Collaboration Begins

My friend eventually thought about it and he started doing it too. He made a distrokid account and we decided to collab so before that album could release worldwide cause I had a specific time for it, we decided to stop it, add two of his songs that he made first, see how it went and we will go from there.

RAGE is Born

The album was hard hitting beats, no vocals, and he came up with two rock and roll instrumentals to put in my song. That Album was Called RAGE. We also added some of his hip hop beats he made as well. It was officially out on March 4th, 2022.

Featured Track: MAFIA

Listen on YouTube

First Taste of Success

That album actually did pretty well and we were both shocked. Not thousands of plays but hundreds for our first album ever and we weren't even that experienced. Our friends were ecstatic, our families got to know about this and of course they were skeptical at first but later they were happy which happened later.

WHAT IF: You hadn't met your collaborator?

Reality

Working with my classmate brought new perspectives, skills, and motivation to my music, helping me reach heights I couldn't have reached alone.

Alternate Path

Without my collaborator, I would still be producing but would have struggled to reach wider audiences. I'd be stuck with tens or hundreds of plays and listeners.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

New Direction

Since we made something that was more towards energy filled beats, we decided to make a slow album or more like I did and I wanted it to have inclusion from drill genre as well. I made some slow drill beats and some slow hip hop beats.

The Whoopty Inspiration

Here is where everything changed for us. I listened to a song called whoopty by CJ and I was huge into drill music and still am. I loved the beat and the melody. I sampled the vocal chop used in that song and I told my friend that I was going to make it into a new drill beat.

"He asked for that sample too and we both made our versions. He came up with an amazing beat and we decided to go for his."

HEARTBREAK Album

We released the album and we couldn't be more shocked. That song went on to become the most streamed song in that album, with over a thousand plays and it was going crazy on YouTube as well. That Album was Called HEARTBREAK and it was released on April 15, 2022, just a month after our first.

Unexpected Success

WHOOPTY Remix exploded in popularity. We were shocked. We were committed and inspired. This was the track that would define our journey and open new doors we never imagined.

Featured Track: WHOOPTY Remix

Listen on YouTube

WHAT IF: You hadn't created Whoopty Remix?

Reality

Creating the WHOOPTY Remix was our breakthrough moment, gaining us over 500K plays and significantly expanding our audience.

Alternate Path

Without our viral hit mixing drill and dance elements, we wouldn't have reached such wide audiences or built the momentum for future releases.

CHALLENGES & GROWTH

Single Releases

After the success of HEARTBREAK, I released a single called LIEz on April 30, 2022, sampling Aliez from Aldnoah Zero and that didn't do well. I admit I got carried away and it didn't turn out good.

Learning from Failure

I was disheartened after the success we saw from HEARTBREAK, to see this flop on my end. I figured it was good cause I got humbled. This was an important lesson in my journey.

The Last of Us

Then we sampled The Last of Us theme and that went well and was released on July 13, 2022. This helped restore some of my confidence after the previous setback.

Featured Track: The Last of Us

Listen on YouTube

Continuous Creation

After that I got busy and released less often but still thrilled to release any way. I continued to produce new music whenever I could find time.

WHAT IF: You had lost motivation after The Last of Us?

Reality

Despite setbacks, I persevered and continued creating music, eventually releasing more albums and growing as an artist.

Alternate Path

I might have taken a break, but my passion for music would have eventually drawn me back. I would likely have asked my collaborator for help to regain momentum.

THE JOURNEY CONTINUES

From RAGE to DRAGON BALL and beyond, my music production journey has been one of growth, challenges, and unexpected success. With 3500 monthly listeners on Spotify, 500K plays of WHOOPTY Remix, and over a million plays on YouTube, what started as curiosity has become something much bigger.

My Promise

I stopped releasing after DRAGON BALL cause I just lost the motivation, I was tired and I had less free time. I was going into my third year. But I planned to release more in the future. Whenever that may be cause I promised myself to never give up and fight through life to do what I truly love.
